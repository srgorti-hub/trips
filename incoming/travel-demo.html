Milngavie to Drymen
Saturday 10 May · Arrive Winnock Hotel
Moderate
📏19.5km
↑260m
⏱5.5hrs

A gentle and sociable opener through farmland and woodland, crossing the Highland Boundary Fault at Conic Hill. The day winds through Mugdock Country Park before opening into rolling lowland pastures — easy underfoot, the perfect warm-up before the trail gets wilder.

Bridge of Orchy to Kingshouse
Thursday 15 May · Across Rannoch Moor · Kingshouse Hotel
Strenuous
📏19km
↑580m
⏱6.5hrs

The most remote and atmospheric day on the entire route. Crossing Rannoch Moor — a vast, treeless, peat-bog wilderness — feels genuinely otherworldly. No shelter, no escape routes, no phone signal for long stretches. The views of Buachaille Etive Mòr on the approach to Kingshouse are among the most dramatic in Scotland.

Open moorland Boggy Exposed No shelter Remote

Elevation Profile
⚠ Key Warnings
  • No reliable water sources for 12 km mid-section — carry 2L minimum
  • No phone signal for most of the moor crossing
  • Weather can change rapidly — full waterproofs essential
  • No escape routes once on the moor — commit to the full crossing

Kingshouse to Kinlochleven
Friday 16 May · The Devil's Staircase · Macdonald Hotel
Strenuous
📏14.5km
↑630m
⏱5hrs

Short in distance but savage in elevation. The climb up the Devil's Staircase — a military road zigzagging up to 548m — is the highest point of the entire route, and rewards with genuinely breathtaking views over Glencoe. A sharp descent leads to the sheltered lochside town of Kinlochleven.

⚠ May-Specific Alerts

Midges: Scottish Highland midges typically emerge in late May, especially in still, humid conditions near Loch Lomond and wooded sections. Carry midge repellent and a head net — non-negotiable in calm weather.

Waterproofs: Do not pack these in your bag transfer — keep them on your person or in your daypack at all times. Weather changes very rapidly, especially above 400m.
